1. Overview and Purpose

This TCPA and Outbound Calling Compliance Policy ("Policy") governs the use of the Propel Dialer and Real Estate AI platform for outbound calling activities. All users of the platform are required to understand and comply with this Policy.

The TCPA (47 U.S.C. § 227) is a federal law that restricts telephone solicitations and the use of automated telephone equipment. The FCC enforces the TCPA, and private individuals may also bring suit for violations. Many states have enacted additional laws that are equal to or stricter than the TCPA.

2. Who This Policy Applies To

This Policy applies to all users of the Propel Dialer platform, including licensed real estate agents and brokers, administrative staff and virtual assistants operating the platform on behalf of a licensee, and any individual or organization with access to the software.

3. The TCPA — What You Need to Know

3.1 Automated Telephone Dialing Systems (ATDS)

Propel Dialer uses automated or pre-programmed dialing features, including sequential contact dialing and automated voicemail drop. Under the TCPA, the use of an ATDS to call cell phones requires prior express written consent from the recipient, unless an exemption applies.

3.2 Pre-Recorded Messages / Voicemail Drops

The voicemail drop feature deposits a pre-recorded message into a contact's voicemail. Before using this feature, you MUST have either:

✓ Prior express written consent from the contact to receive pre-recorded messages; OR an established business relationship (EBR) — note: EBR exemptions are limited and state-specific. Consult an attorney.

3.3 The National Do Not Call Registry

You may NOT call numbers registered on the National DNC Registry unless you have a prior established business relationship (within 18 months of a transaction or 3 months of an inquiry), or you have the person's express written consent.

⚠ You must scrub your contact lists against the National DNC Registry at minimum every 31 days and honor internal DNC requests within 30 days.

3.4 Calling Hours Restrictions

Federal law prohibits telephone solicitations before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM local time at the called party's location. Many states have stricter restrictions. You are responsible for verifying the time zone of each contact and observing state-specific restrictions.

4. Required Consent Standards

4.1 Prior Express Written Consent (PEWC)

For calls using an ATDS or pre-recorded messages to cell phones, the FCC requires a signed written agreement (paper or electronic) that clearly discloses authorization for calls using ATDS or pre-recorded messages to the specific telephone number provided. The agreement must NOT be required as a condition of purchase.

⚠ A verbal "yes" is NOT sufficient for PEWC. The agreement must be in writing.

4.2 Real Estate Exemptions and Limitations

  • The EBR exemption does NOT apply to cell phones for ATDS calls or pre-recorded messages
  • The EBR exemption is limited in duration (18 months from last transaction, 3 months from inquiry)
  • For cold calling expired listings and FSBOs, ensure the number is not on the DNC Registry
  • State-specific mini-TCPA laws may impose additional requirements

5. Required Internal Policies

5.1 Written Do Not Call Policy

You must maintain a written DNC policy covering your DNC list procedures, how you process opt-out requests, and how you train staff on DNC compliance.

5.2 Internal Do Not Call List

Honor DNC requests within 30 days and maintain your internal DNC list for a minimum of 5 years. The Propel Dialer DNC feature assists with this, but you are responsible for its proper use.

5.3 Consent Records

Maintain records of consent for each contact called using automated features, including: date consent was obtained, method (form, email, etc.), specific number consented to, and content of the consent disclosure.

5.4 Call Recording Disclosure

  • One-party consent states (e.g., Maryland): Only one party needs to consent to recording
  • Two-party / all-party consent states (e.g., California, Florida): ALL parties must consent before recording

You must identify which state's law governs each call and provide required disclosures. Failure to comply with state wiretapping laws can result in criminal and civil liability.

6. Calling Script Compliance

When making outbound calls, you must: identify yourself by name at the beginning of the call; state the name of your real estate business/brokerage; provide a phone number or address where you can be reached; honor any do-not-call request immediately; and not use deceptive or misleading practices.

7. Prohibited Practices

The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Calling numbers on the National DNC Registry without required exemption or consent
  • Calling before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM local time
  • Leaving pre-recorded voicemails without required consent
  • Calling any number after the contact has requested DNC placement
  • Using false or misleading caller ID information (Truth in Caller ID Act)
  • Using deceptive or abusive calling practices
  • Making calls for purposes other than legitimate real estate business

8. State-Specific Requirements

The following states have telemarketing laws that may be stricter than federal TCPA: California (all-party recording consent; CPUC regulations), Florida (Florida Telemarketing Act), Texas (Business and Commerce Code Ch. 305), New York (Do Not Call Law), and Washington (Automatic Dialing regulations). This list is not exhaustive — consult an attorney for each state where your contacts are located.

9. Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Statutory damages of $500 per violation (negligent) or $1,500 per violation (willful)
  • Class action lawsuits (a single campaign can result in millions in liability)
  • FCC enforcement actions and fines
  • State attorney general enforcement actions
  • Real estate license disciplinary action
  • Reputational damage
